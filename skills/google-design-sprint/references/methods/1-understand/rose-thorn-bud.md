# Rose, Thorn, Bud

Rose, Thorn, Bud is a technique for identifying things as positive, negative, or having potential. Members of the Boy Scouts of America are taught to be thorough, methodical, and analytical about each situation they encounter. In turn, scouts are routinely encouraged to identify one positive experience (Rose), one negative experience (Thorn), and one new goal or insight (Bud). Adapted for use as a design method, this structure provides an opportunity to analyze a set of data or help scope a problem by revealing focus areas, allowing you to plan next steps. You can frame your ensuing activities by documenting observations or opinions on sticky notes as positive, negative, or having potential.

* **Time:** 10-30 mins
* **Activity:** Individual
* **Output:** Color-coded observations for later synthesis.

### Directions

1. Identify a topic for consideration.
2. Assemble a diverse group of stakeholders.
3. Give each participant a pen and 3 sticky note pads.
4. Explain the topic and the color key.
5. Rose = Pink (indicates things that are positive).
6. Thorn = Blue (indicates things that are negative).
7. Bud = Green (indicates things that have potential).
8. Instruct each person to generate many data points.
9. Include one issue, insight, or idea per sticky note. Tell participants to write multiple items per color.
