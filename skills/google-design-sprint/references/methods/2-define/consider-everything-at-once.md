# Consider Everything At Once

This visualization technique of having everything in front of us at once can be of great value when we think about creative synthesis in Design Sprints. This method, Consider Everything At Once, starts with take-aways and inspirational quotes gathered in the Understand Phase, and digests these research efforts structurally, intuitively and concisely. This method is designed to foster critical assessment by the team and bring visual sense. Research conducted in the field of psychology and decision-making indicates that even if it seems odd to think about the results this way, there is strong evidence for the positive effect of considering everything at once.

* **Time:** 30-50 mins
* **Activity:** Group
* **Output:** Four-quadrant synthesis board.
* **Source:** Norman Vogel (Fiducia & GAD IT AG)

### Directions

1. Create 4 quadrants on a whiteboard.
2. Fill the top left quadrant with your understandings from various Understand sources. Visualize them concisely. Order notes in the first quadrant if necessary.
3. Discuss and name recurring themes. Add these themes to the top right quadrant.
4. In the bottom left quadrant, write down the hunches that occur along the process.
5. In the bottom right quadrant, write down the perspective-changing insight statements from the group.
6. The group can add and change things in each quadrant as needed.
7. As you iterate, feed your new outputs back in.
