# Action Planning

Action planning is a simple method for teams participating in a sprint to identify concrete, time-bound actions they intend to take as a result of the sprint’s learnings. This method can greatly magnify a sprint’s real-world impact by encouraging sprint participants to socialize learnings with specific stakeholders at specific times, and by creating shared accountability for follow-up actions. This exercise can be a useful add-on to sprints that are geared towards creating a specific deliverable, but might have implications for other teams and initiatives. Action planning helps sprint participants identify other steps they can take to socialize new learnings and magnify a sprint’s reach and impact. Note that all planned actions should be as specific as possible.

* **Time:** 10 mins
* **Activity:** Group
* **Output:** Time-bound action items and accountability partners.
* **Source:** Sudden Compass®

### Directions

1. Pair up with somebody else who is participating in your sprint. This person is your “accountability partner”.
2. Review each other’s action items, and create shared calendar events for the date specified on each action item.
3. Creating shared calendar items is a simple step that reinforces future-facing accountability amongst sprint teams.
4. When the shared calendar event is triggered, email your accountability partner with an update on your action item, or to inquire about their action item.
